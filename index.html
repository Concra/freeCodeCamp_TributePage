Benjamin Franklin

Image of Benjamin Franklin, sitting, for a portrait in the mid-1780s

  • Born on January 17, 1706, Franklin lived to be 84 years old; passing on April 17, 1790
  • He was known as a polymath (or person who's knowledge and expertise covers several areas and pulls from them to solve problems
  • The first president of the Academy and College of Philadelphia (now Univerity of Philadelphia)
  • Franklin never patented his inventions believing that "we should be glad of an opportunity to serve others by any invention of ours"
  • Became Grand Master of the Free-Masons in 1734, three years after joining.
  • After being promoted to deputy postmaster-general of the British colonies in 1753, Franklin was able to setup the first national communications network
  • The first United States Postmaster General, during the American Revolution
  • He studied Altantic Ocean currents during his time as deputy postmaster and published the Gulf Stream chart in 1770.
    • After recognition and adoption, sea captians were able to reduce their trans-Atlantic trips by two weeks.
